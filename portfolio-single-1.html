Google Maps Business Management System

A System tailored for bussiness owners to manage reviews and business informations on Google Maps.

My Role

UI/UX Design

Company

Flashaim

Due to the convenience and popularity of Google Maps, ratings and reviews of the shop are increasingly used as a reference for consumers' willingness to make purchases, and business owners have come to realize the importance of managing Google My Business informations on Google Maps.

The native Google My Business management system does not provide as many managing tools, it would be difficult and inefficient for larger business owners with franchisees to manage all the shops.

It integrates and manages different Google My Business accounts and strengthens management tools of products, comments, post scheduling, map ordering, and etc., allowing authorized management accounts to easily do so through the system.

This system extends Google's functions is therefore designed with the guideline of Google material design system, allowing users to make the transition with easse and maintaining consistency.

Problems solved

1.
Different accounts needs to login to the native system separately, to update informations requires many steps.

Integrate and manage different accounts to better understand the rating status of all shops. It can also be categorized into groups to facilitate settings for different groups.

2.
Hard to manage and reply a large number of comments.

Since negative reviews may affect customers' impressions of a shop, if a negative review is responded in real time, this effectively improves the impression. The system filters review scores all on the same page, replying comments simultaneously can be done easily, without having login into separate accounts and reply individually.

3.
No ordering or reservation functions on native system.

The original system does not have order and reservation functions. This is an additional function of the system to allow business owners to have an additional platform to grow customers from the map.
